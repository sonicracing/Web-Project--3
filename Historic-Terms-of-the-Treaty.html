Location of Treaty Terms of Treaty Communities

Historic Terms of the Treaty:

Land: Treaty 5 guaranteed reserves for the signatory First Nations, with each family of five typically receiving a 160 acre land allocation.

Annuities: Indigenous individuals were entitled to annual payments of $5 a year from the government.

Hunting and Fishing Rights: Indigenous peoples retained the right to hunt, fish, and trap on their traditional territories.

Education: The treaty provided for education services, but this aspect has been a source of historical contention due to the residential school system.

How is Treaty 5 different

The treaty is different because instead of dealing with a large area at one time, the band commisioner went to each regional band and did it that way.
